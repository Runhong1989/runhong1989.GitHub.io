Differential Geometry III - Complex Geometry

We will explain the basic concepts of complex structures, Kahler structures, projective varieties, holomorphic bundles and currents. We will cover important theorems such as Hodge decomposition, Hirzebruch-Riemann-Roch, Kodaira vanishing, Kodaira embedding and Hormander’s estimate. An introduction to the deformation theory and moduli spaces will be given so that you can see how various results of the course can be applied. We will rely heavily on the following tools: sheaf theory, linear elliptic PDE’s and complex analysis. If time permits we will also introduce some nonlinear methods.

Preliminaries
Algebraic topology, differential manifolds, Riemannian geometry, complex analysis, real analysis, functional analysis, partial differential equations, abstract algebra.
