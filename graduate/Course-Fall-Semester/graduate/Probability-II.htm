Probability Theory - II

The course is designed for senior undergraduate students and graduates students of math major. Topics covered include:

  1. Measure theory (Caratheodory extension theorem, product measure, Kolmogorov extension theorem, Radon-Nikodym theorem, convergence of probabilities on metric space) .
  2. Law of large numbers, central limit theorem.
  3. Conditional (mathematical) expectation and its properties.
  4. Brownian motions and limit distributions.
  5. Martingales.
  6. Random walks and Markov Chains.
  7. Ergodic theorem.

Prerequisites
Probability Theory (I) (MAT 40420624) and Measure and Integrals (MAT 30420334) or equivalent.

Textbooks and Reference books

  1. Rick Durrett, Probability: Theory and Examples.
  2. M. Loeve , Probability Theory (II) .
  3. Jian Yan, Lecture on measure theory.
