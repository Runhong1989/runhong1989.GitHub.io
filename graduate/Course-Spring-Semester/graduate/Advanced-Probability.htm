Chapter II. Brownian motion on manifolds
After a brief introduction to the Brownian motion from a physicist’s point of view, and a short but rigorous construction of this fundamental stochastic process, we will explain how the Brownian motion can be defined on smooth curved spaces, and used to study them. We will see on some examples that the asymptotic behaviour of the Brownian motion reflects the geometry of the space on which it lives. We will then discuss informally the wonderful fact that the Brownian motion, through the interplay between its small-time and long-time behaviour, gives an analytical hand on the delicate relations between the local and global properties of the space on which it lives. There exists for example a Brownian motion-based proof of the venerable Gauss-Bonnet formula.
