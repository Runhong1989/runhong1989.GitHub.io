Riemannian Geometry

This a course for graduate students and some senior undergraduate students of math major. Topics covered include: Riemannian manifolds, Levi-Civita connection. Riemannian Geodesic, the exponential map, normal neighborhood and normal coordinates, completeness and Hopf-Rinow theorem. Curvatures, sectional curvature, Ricci curvature, scalar curvature, tensors. Jacobi fields, Jabobi equation, conjugate points, computations of Jacobi fields. Isometric immersion, submanifold, the second fundamental form, fundamental equations. Complete manifolds, Hopf-Rinow and Hadamard Theorems. Space of constant curvature, Theorem of Cartan on the determination of the metric by means of curvature, hyperbolic space, space forms.

The Gauss-Bonnet theorem. Variations of energy, the first and the second variational formulas of energy; Bonnet-Myers’ Theorem and Synge’s Theorem. Morse index Theorem. The fundamental group of manifolds of negative curvature. The Rauch comparison theorem. Sphere theorem.

Text book and references

  1. M. P. Do Carmo, Riemannian Geometry, 1992.
  2. John M. Lee, Riemannian manifolds, GTM 176, 1997.
