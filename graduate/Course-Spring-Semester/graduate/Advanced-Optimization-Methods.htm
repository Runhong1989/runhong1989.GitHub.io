Advanced Optimization Methods

This is an introductory course designed for senior undergraduate students and graduate students who are interested in determinant computational methods of optimization. The preliminary courses of linear algebra, calculus and linear programming are needed.

After introducing the combinatorial optimization problems and the complexity theory, we first give some meta-heuristics like tabu search, simulated annealing, genetic algorithm, ant colony optimization algorithm and neural networks etc. We focus on their methodology of modeling and mathematical theory. Then Lagrangian duality model is introduced to provide lower bounds for the combinatorial optimization problems. Finally, linear conic optimization models like second-order conic programming, semi-definite positive programming etc are briefly listed as tools for finding global optimization solution or lower bounds for the optimization problems.
