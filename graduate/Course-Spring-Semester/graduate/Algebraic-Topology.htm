Algebraic Topology

The goal of this course is to cover the basics on homology, cohomology and homotopy theory. Topics include

Homology theory

  1. Singular homology, construction.
  2. Chain complexes, chain maps, chain homotopies; exact sequences; the Euler characteristic.
  3. Homotopy invariance of singular homology.
  4. Relative homology, long exact sequence of a pair.
  5. The excision theorem.
  6. Equivalence of simplicial and singular homology.
  7. Homology of CW-complexes via cellular homology.
  8. Computations: surfaces, spheres, projective spaces, lens spaces, etc.
  9. Mayer-Vietoris sequence, more calculations.
  10. Applications: Brouwer fixed point theorem, degrees, Jordan curve theorem, invariance of domain, etc.
  11. Eilenberg-Steenrod axioms.

Cohomology theory

  1. Singular cohomology groups.
  2. Universal coefficient theorem.
  3. Relative cohomology, exact sequences of a pair.
  4. Cup product, calculations (cohomology ring of projective spaces, etc).
  5. Kunneth formula.
  6. Cap product, Orientation of manifolds, Poincare duality.
  7. Intersection number, the Lefschetz fixed-point theorem.
