Game Theory and its Applications

This course introduces the basic game-theoretic models and their applications in various fields such as economics, marketing and operations management. Four basic non-cooperative game-theoretic models (static game with complete information, dynamic game with complete information, static game with incomplete information, dynamic game with incomplete information) are analyzed, including the definition, the property and the computation methods for their corresponding equilibria. Cooperation games, evolutionary games and behavioral games are also briefly discussed. For their applications, this course introduces mechanism design and supply chain coordination etc., including their mathematical models and theoretical analyses.

Description of classes
Four 45-minute lectures per week.

Notes
Apart from the homework assignments, students are also asked to read and summarize some recently-published journal papers or do a course project.

Placement and Prerequisites
This course is for math major graduates and senior undergraduates. Courses such as Calculus (or Mathematical Analysis), Linear Algebra, and Probability Theory are required.

FAQ
Homework Assignments 30%, Readings or Project 20%, Final Exam 50%.
