Stochastic Processes

This course is designed for senior undergraduate students and graduate students. Topics covered include: Basic concepts of stochastic processes, Poisson processes and Brownian motions, Markov chains, Markov processes, martingale processes, stationary processes, renewal processes and Markov renewal processes, cumulative processes, Levy processes and diffusion processes.

Prerequisites
Probability Theory (II) (70420264) and Probability Theory (I) (40420624); or equivalent.

Textbooks and reference books

  1. Richard F. Bass, Stochastic processes.
  2. J. Medhi, Stochastic processes.

Description of classes
Classes meet Tuesday and Friday.

Notes
Some reading and 6-12 homework problems per week which will account for 50% of the course grade.

Course Grade
Final term exam 50%, Homework Problems 50%.
