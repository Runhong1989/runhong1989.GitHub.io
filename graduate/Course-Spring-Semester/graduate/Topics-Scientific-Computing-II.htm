Topics in Scientific Computing - II

This course is on frontier topics in scientific computing. Topics that are covered in this course are not fixed.

  1. Numerical continuation methods for nonlinear systems compute approximate solutions of a system of parameterized nonlinear equations. Mathematical software AUTO will be used.
    References
    1. J. F. Govaerts, "Numerical Methods for Bifurcations of Dynamical Equilibria", SIAM 2000.

  2. Graph sparsification algorithms are developed by D Spielman, a Yale professor. He wrote a large paper on this topic, "Nearly-Linear Time Algorithms for Graph Partitioning, Graph Sparsification and Solving Linear Systems" (STOC 04) in which he showed how to solve symmetric, diagonally-dominant linear systems in nearly-linear time.
    References
    1. D. Spielman, Shang-Hua Teng , Spectral Sparsification of Graphs, SICOMP 2011.
    2. D. Spielman,Srivastava , Graph Sparsification by Effective Resistances, (STOC 08).

  3. Nonnegative Matrix Factorization (NMF) is a group algorithms in numerical linear algebra. Matrix V is factorized into (usually) two matrices W and H, with the property that all three matrices have no negative elements.
    References
    1. Daniel D. Lee and Daniel D. Lee and H. Sebastian Seung (1999). "Learning the parts of objects by non-negative matrix factorization". Nature 401 (6755): 788--791 (1999).

Prerequisite
Advanced Numerical Analysis.
