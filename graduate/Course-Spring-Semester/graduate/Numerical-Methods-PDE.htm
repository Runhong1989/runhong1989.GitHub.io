Numerical Methods for Partial Differential Equations

Description of the Course: Introduction to the fundamentals of numerical techniques for the linear and nonlinear elliptic, parabolic and hyperbolic partial differential equations which have a wide variety of applications in science, engineering, and many other applied disciplines. Topics include: Finite Element Discretization; Finite Difference Discretization; Spectral Discretization and Operator Splitting technique; Direct and Iterative Solution Methods. Lectures are supplemented with numerical experiments using MATLAB. This course is designed for upperclass undergraduates interested in the applied mathematics. Students are expected to running numerical simulations assigned as homework problems.

Prerequisites
Some introductory level courses of Partial Differential Equations, Functional Analysis, Numerical Analysis or equivalent.

Textbooks

  2. Stig Larsson and Vidar Thomee: Partial Differential Equations with Numerical Methods, Berlin; New York; Springer, 2009.

  2. W.L. Briggs, V.E. Henson, S.F. McCormick: A Multigrid Tutorial, Tsinghua University Press, SIAM, 2011.
